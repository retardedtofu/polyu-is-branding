01 · Story

Why BDSIS exists

The brand story is a five-act argument. Use it whole in talks and films; use single acts in posts and posters. It always ends in the same place: the mission.

Act 1 · The doubt

What can't we learn online?

In 2016 we asked "what can we learn online?" In 2026 we ask "what can't we learn online?" Degrees are questioned, careers are fragmenting into slashers and one-person start-ups, and AI is levelling up — eating jobs one by one. So what should we learn now?

Act 2 · The shift

Interdisciplinary is the natural consequence

Each industrial revolution expanded an individual's reach; AI is the fourth and fastest. Greater reach makes other domains relevant — engineering meets business, AI meets finance, hospitality meets psychology. New discoveries happen at the interfaces (OECD 2018; EUA 2021).

Act 3 · The catch

Knowledge is not enough

Imagine the perfectly interdisciplinary young CEO. What's still missing is trust in their judgement — and judgement is wisdom, experience and maturity: a deep, personal understanding of human behaviour, needs and aspirations.

Act 4 · The stakes

The human foundation is at risk

Humanity's superpower is co-operating flexibly in large numbers — enabled by storytelling. First we lost our attention to social media; now we are losing storytelling to AI. The foundation of human society is at great risk.

Act 5 · The answer

BDSIS educates leaders who are interdisciplinary enough for a technology-driven world — and human enough to keep it human-centric.

Vision

Two complementary dimensions

Technology dimensionHuman dimension
Technology makes the world more interdisciplinary, with new and more complex challenges. Future leaders need interdisciplinary knowledge, skills, mindsets — and self-learning skills. Human needs, aspirations and the foundations of society never change. Future leaders need deep human understanding and strong soft skills to shape and sustain human-centric societies.
Positioning

An upgrade of any PolyU programme

BDSIS does not sit beside PolyU's programmes; it sits above them. Any subject in any faculty can become part of a BDSIS self-determined curriculum. Two moves define the position:

Vertical — elite

Highest entry requirement in PolyU. ~30 students, recruited globally. Mentors drawn from the university's most accomplished scholars and senior management.

Horizontal — universal

The full breadth of ~10 faculties and schools as raw material. 19 founding students built 19 different degrees — the focus is printed in each degree title.

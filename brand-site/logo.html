03 · Logo

The "iS" monogram

A 3×3 grid: eight quarter-circle slices and one square split into two triangles compose the letters "iS" — distinct coloured cells assembling into one form. The logo is the brand argument: disciplines connecting into something greater.

Primary symbol — full colour on white

Anatomy

The "i" is the warm left column — purple, crimson, orange. The "S" is the cool right — greens above, blues below. Warm meets cool exactly as the brand core does: Humans × Technology.

Gradients always run within one hue family. The symbol is never redrawn, recoloured, rotated or given new cells.

Variants

One symbol, four colourways

Original — default on white / light
White — on ink, photos, gradients
Greyscale — mono print
Black — single-colour repro

Each variant ships as CMYK (.ai / .pdf / .jpg) and RGB (.png, transparent) in the logo pack.

Lockups

Programme-name lockups

Horizontal — headers, banners, co-branding rows
Vertical — covers, posters, square formats

The lockup is trilingual by construction: English name in small-caps blue, a thin rule, then 跨學科組合學士課程 in green with · BDSIS in orange. Horizontal and vertical each exist in two proportions (1 & 2) and three colourways (Original / White / Black).

Rules

Usage

Do

  • Use Original on white or near-white; White on ink, deep colour or photography
  • Keep clear space of at least the height of one grid cell (¼ of symbol height) on all sides
  • Minimum size: 24 px / 8 mm symbol height; use the lockup only where the name is legible
  • Pair with PolyU + CUS marks in the endorsed footer arrangement (see Applications)

Don't

  • Recolour, reflow or add cells to the monogram; never flatten the gradients to solids
  • Place Original on busy photography or mid-tone colour — switch to White
  • Stretch, rotate, outline, or add drop shadows
  • Set the programme name in your own type next to the bare symbol — use the supplied lockups

Clear-space and minimum-size values above are working recommendations pending designer confirmation — the source .ai files contain no documented rules.

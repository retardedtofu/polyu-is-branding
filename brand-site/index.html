Humans ×
Technology.

The brand of the Bachelor's Degree Scheme in Interdisciplinary Studies (BDSIS) — PolyU's elite programme — is built on one tension: technology is dissolving the boundaries between disciplines, while human judgement, needs and society remain the constant. BDSIS graduates master both. Everything in this guide expresses that idea.

The mission

"Nurturing future leaders to shape and sustain human-centric societies with rapidly evolving technology."

Every claim the brand makes — elite admission, one-on-one mentorship, the self-determined curriculum — exists to serve this sentence.

How the core works

One tension, two registers

The brand always holds both sides of its core idea. You will see the duality everywhere — in leadership (a social-scientist Dean paired with an engineer Programme Director), in the logo (a warm "i", a cool "S"), and in this guide's own typography.

Human · warm

Judgement, empathy, storytelling, society.

Expressed in the warm half of the spectrum, the serif voice, first-person student stories, and people-first photography.

Technology · cool

Systems, precision, interdisciplinary structure, AI-era fluency.

Expressed in the cool half of the spectrum, the grotesk display type, exact numbers and grid-based layouts.

The brand in one paragraph

For top-performing students in Hong Kong, the Mainland and worldwide, BDSIS is PolyU's elite, fully personalised bachelor's scheme — an upgrade of any PolyU programme. Students design a self-determined curriculum across every faculty, guided one-on-one by the university's most accomplished scholars, and graduate with their personal focus printed in their degree title. Nineteen students in the founding cohort; nineteen different degrees. Breaking barriers, empowering future leaders.

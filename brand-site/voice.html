02 · Voice

How the brand speaks

Confident enough to claim "most prestigious"; human enough to mean it. The voice holds the same tension as the brand core — precise about mechanics, idealistic about purpose.

The tagline

BREAKING BRRIERS, EMPWERING FUTURE LEAERS.

Always all-caps, always with the full stop. Three letters are replaced by shapes from the logo: the A becomes a triangle (orange→red), the O a circle (green), the D a half-circle (blue). Use the supplied lockup artwork — do not rebuild by hand. No Chinese version exists yet; commission one rather than translating ad hoc.

Voice attributes

Five rules of the voice

RuleIn practice
Confident, not boastfulState superlatives as administrable facts: "highest entry score requirement in PolyU", "world's most-cited 2% scientists". Every big claim travels with its proof.
Question firstOpen with tension — "What can't we learn online?" — then resolve. Provocation is for attention, never for cynicism.
Big-picture and idealisticGlobal challenges, human-centric societies, future leaders. Quote authorities (OECD, EUA) when the idealism needs grounding.
Personal, student-first"Your aspirations", "self-determined", "one-on-one". Students speak in first person; mentors are named people, not titles.
Precise about mechanicsCredits, codes and percentages are exact: 34.5, Top 2%, 54 Major credits. Precision is part of the elite promise.

House style: British English (programme, personalised, enrol). Terminology: use Personal Academic Mentor (PAM) everywhere — retire "Personal Coach".

Message house

Four pillars, each with proof

Pillar 1

Elite by design

Highest entry bar in PolyU (DSE 34.5 · IB 40 · 3A*+1A · Gaokao top 2%). ~30 students, globally recruited. Direct support from the President and Vice Presidents.

Pillar 2

Truly interdisciplinary

A self-determined curriculum across every PolyU faculty, structured by three pathways and the CUS101→401 core. Proof: 19 students, 19 different degrees.

Pillar 3

Radically personalised

A renowned scholar as one-on-one Personal Academic Mentor; a four-tier advising structure; your personal focus printed in your degree title.

Pillar 4

A privileged experience

Guaranteed hall residence for two years, waived human-skills workshop fees, enhanced overseas opportunities — and a safety net: switch back to a regular programme in Year 2.

"I chose Hospitality Management and Psychology… my Personal Academic Mentor is Professor Kaye Chon, a leading scholar and Dean of the SHTM. He always shares his valuable life experiences with me."Lyra, Year 1 — testimonial format: photo · first-person quote · "Name, Year | Study Focus: X + Y"

Each student designs a self-determined curriculum across all PolyU faculties, mentored one-on-one by the university's most accomplished scholars — graduating with a personal focus named in their degree title.

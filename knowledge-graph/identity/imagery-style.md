# Imagery Style

## Photography subjects observed

1. **Campus architecture** — PolyU's red-brick campus, the Jockey Club Innovation Tower, the "POLYU" sign lawn; establishes place and institutional weight (brochure pp.1-3).
2. **Mentorship moments** — professor and student one-on-one in bright modern interiors (p.6); the core promise visualised ([[mentorship-and-advising]]).
3. **Community/occasion** — group photo with senior management at the Chinese New Year gathering, banner and decorations visible (p.7).
4. **Student portraits** — individual students, natural light, casual smart dress, holding programme materials; paired with first-person quotes ([[student-stories]]).

## Treatment

- Photography is frequently **screened behind gradient color** — blended into the quadrant grid ([[graphic-devices]]) with blue/warm gradient overlays (cover, p.2, p.4) so it reads as part of the color system rather than competing with it.
- Untreated photos are reserved for **people**: mentorship and student portraits stay true-colour and warm.
- Captions in small grey type under a thin rule.

## Implied rules for the guideline

- Overlay photography with brand-gradient washes when used as background; keep people-photography clean when used as content.
- Imagery should show *real students and real mentors* (named) — authenticity is part of the elite promise.

# Graphic Devices

The identity has a coherent geometric system. Four devices recur:

## 1 · The quadrant grid ("super-graphic")

- A grid of square cells filled with the brand's gradient families, with **one corner cell rounded into a quarter-circle** — a magnified fragment of the [[logo]].
- Used as: brochure cover art, page-2 full-bleed background (blended over photography), back-cover layout grid, side strips on inner pages (thin vertical columns of gradient cells with one rounded end).
- Cells can hold flat gradients, photography, or text panels — it doubles as a **layout system**.

## 2 · Triangle mosaic background

- Light, airy field of right-triangle half-squares in **ice blue** tones with occasional green/orange/red accent triangles; fades to white in the centre (`Background.png`, used in both Online Banners and Online Graphic).
- Role: a quiet texture for digital banners that keeps content legible while continuing the geometric language.

## 3 · Letter-replacement shapes

- Triangle (orange→red), circle (green), half-circle D (blue) replacing A / O / D in the [[tagline]] lockup. The same trio could extend to other headline words — a campaignable device.

## 4 · Gradient line-icon illustration

- The 1080×1080 online graphic: thin-line icons (DNA strand, microchip, cloud + pen-tool, finance/coins + chart, gears/monitor) in **gradient strokes**, each framed in a soft shape, **connected by circuit-like lines with node dots** — disciplines literally wired together.
- Establishes an icon/illustration style: outline weight, gradient strokes, connector lines, rounded frames.

## System logic

Everything derives from **circle + square + triangle on a grid, filled with spectrum gradients** = many simple parts composing a richer whole — the visual argument for interdisciplinarity ([[brand-essence]]).

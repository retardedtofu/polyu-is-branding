# Mentorship and Advising

The single most differentiating feature of BDSIS ([[key-messages]] Pillar 3).

## Personal Academic Mentor (PAM)

- **Every student is matched one-on-one** with a renowned PolyU professor in an area related to their aspirations.
- Mentor calibre (brochure p.6): *"the most accomplished internationally renowned scholars in PolyU… including the world's most cited 2% scientists by Stanford University and Clarivate, Chair Professors, and/or scholars with outstanding achievements."* Includes senior management of the university.
- What PAMs provide (deck slide 46): one-on-one guidance on aspirations and pathway to success; disciplinary/interdisciplinary trends, opportunities, challenges; sharing experience and wisdom.
- Matching considers academic interests and potential study areas; PAMs review study plans **at the end of each semester** (deck slide 55).
- Contrast line (deck slide 47): *"Regular Hospitality or Computer Science students have no one-on-one access to accomplished professors or senior management of university."*

### Named examples ([[leadership-and-people]])

- **Prof. Kaye Chon**, Dean of School of Hotel and Tourism Management → mentor to Lyra (Hospitality + Psychology)
- **Prof. Hongxia Yang**, Associate Dean, Faculty of Computational and Mathematical Science; ex-Head of LLMs at ByteDance US; ex-Director of Alibaba DAMO Academy; CoinDesk World Top 50 Women in AI and Web3 → mentor to Yiran (AI + FinTech)

## Four-tier advising structure (brochure p.6)

1. **Dean of College of Undergraduate Studies** — higher-level advice, understands personal character (deck mentions MBTI) and aspirations; mobilises university support
2. **BDSIS Programme Leader** — initial guidance before PAM matching; curriculum consultation
3. **Personal Academic Mentor (PAM)**
4. **Undergraduate Studies Support (USS) Team** — logistical and administrative support

(The deck's earlier "3 layers" — slide 45 — became four tiers in the brochure; brochure version is current.)

## Beyond advising

Direct support from PolyU Senior Management including the **President and Vice Presidents**: regular meetings, engagement sessions, orientation dinner, Chinese New Year gathering (brochure p.7; deck slide 48).

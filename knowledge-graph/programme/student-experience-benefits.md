# Student Experience & Benefits

## Exclusive benefits vs regular PolyU programmes (deck slide 50)

- **Guaranteed hall residence for the first two years** — University-managed Accommodation (UMA: student halls and off-campus housing). Regular undergraduates are entitled to ONE year only, not guaranteed. If BDSIS students enrol in the **Undergraduate Research and Innovation Scheme (URIS)** in Y2/Y3, they are admitted to **CURI Residential College** under the URIS offer (brochure p.7).
- **Human-skills courses/workshops by the Student Affairs Office: enrolment fee waived** (others pay).
- **Enhanced overseas trips and experiences** — service learning, exchange, internships, general-education courses (others: standard).
- **Option to switch back** to a regular programme in Year 2, subject to approval — the parent-facing safety net.
- **Scholarships** — mentioned in Samuel's testimonial ([[student-stories]]); details not specified in supplied materials (gap).

## Human skills development (deck slide 49)

"Learning opportunities are not restricted to classrooms, formal lectures and learning credits" — workshops, dinners, engagement with senior management ([[mentorship-and-advising]]). This operationalises the human/soft-skills half of the [[brand-essence]] vision.

# Award Designations

## The degree format (deck slide 44)

> *"XXX has been awarded the degree of BACHELOR OF SCIENCE/ARTS/ENG IN INTERDISCIPLINARY STUDIES **with a focus in** Sustainable Urban Design, with First Class Honours."*

The **personal focus is named in the degree** — determined in consultation with the Programme Leader. Award designations available: **BA, BBA, BEng, BSc**.

## Study plan shapes (deck slide 39)

1. Interdisciplinary Major involving two disciplines — e.g. *BSc (Hons) in Interdisciplinary Studies (with a focus on Applied Mathematics and Computing)*
2. Interdisciplinary Major **with a Minor** — e.g. …, *Minor in Research and Innovation*
3. Interdisciplinary Major **with Secondary Major** — e.g. …, *Secondary Major in Artificial Intelligence and Data Analytics*

## Governing principles (deck slides 53-54)

- Minimum **120 credits** for graduation; WIE internship counts as training credits, excluded from the 120.
- Minimum **54 Major credits**, including the four Interdisciplinary Core subjects + self-determined curriculum ([[curriculum]]).
- For a specific designation (BA/BBA/BEng/BSc): **≥60% of Major credits within that designation**, normally **≥45 Major credits**, including the 6-credit Capstone (CUS401) but excluding the other 12 Interdisciplinary Core credits (CUS101, CUS202, CUS201/301).
  - Calculation: 60% × (120 − 12 core − 27 GUR − 6 free electives/Minor) = 45.
- Guided/Independent Studies subjects (for areas not offered at PolyU) count toward Major credits.

## Operations (deck slide 55)

Tentative designation proposed at admission → Personal Coach + Undergraduate Studies Support (USS) team guide subject selection (Programme Leader covers the gap before coach matching) → Dean of CUS / Programme Leader advise when necessary → Personal Coaches review study plans **end of each semester**.

> Note: deck slides 55 use "Personal Coach"; the brochure standardises on "Personal Academic Mentor (PAM)" — see [[mentorship-and-advising]]. Terminology should be unified in the guideline.

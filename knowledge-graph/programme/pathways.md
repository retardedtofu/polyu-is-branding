# Pathways — Self-determined Curriculum

Two starting modes (deck slide 52): **start with a focus and build the study around it**, or **start without a focus and work towards one**. Either way, students choose one of three pathways and converge on a specific focus tackled in the Interdisciplinary Capstone ([[curriculum]]).

## Pathway A — Choose from 1–3 strands

Strands (adapted from **UCL's pathways**, per deck notes):

1. **Cultures and Societies** — depts incl. AF, APSS, SD, SHTM, SFT, CBS, CHC, ENGL, LMS, MM
2. **Sciences and Engineering** — AAE, ABCT, AMA, AP, BEEE, BME, BRE, CEE, COMP, DSAI, EEE, ISE, ME
3. **Health and Environment** — BEEE, BME, CEE, FSN, HTI, LSGI, RS, SN, SO

Select subjects from the catalogue in consultation with Programme Leader / Personal Academic Mentor.

## Pathway B — Integrating known fields

Integrated study of 2–3 existing subject disciplines; select subjects from 2–3 majors (e.g. Hospitality + Psychology — see Lyra in [[student-stories]]).

## Pathway C — Solving world problems

Tailor-made study of a major local/national/regional/global issue (e.g. **UNESCO SDG**). Select subjects from any department — *can include a few from other universities*.

## Example discipline combinations (deck slide 10 — useful for marketing copy)

Engineering + Business · Hospitality + Psychology · AI + FinTech · Economics + Sociology · Nursing + Medical Tech · Green Finance + Power Engineering · Material Science + AI · International Relations + Poverty

## Branding note

The three-pathway structure is the answer to the "is self-determined just unstructured?" objection — freedom with scaffolding. Worth a dedicated diagram in the guideline ([[brand-guideline-outline]]).

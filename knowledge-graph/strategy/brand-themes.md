# Dual Brand Themes — Prestige × Stewardship

## The brief (leadership, verbatim intent)

Explore **two complementary branding themes**: (1) elite, prestige; (2) nurturing future leaders that society can depend on — *"we want to train more Amodei and not Altman."* I.e. trustworthy, principled power, not ambition at any cost.

## The synthesis

> **Elite is the duty, not the reward.** Selection is where obligation begins.

Not two messages to balance — one sentence. The bar proves the talent ([[admissions]]); the programme builds the character that makes talent trustworthy. This was already latent in [[brand-essence]] ("human-centric societies") and in the deck's own claim that *judgement is the most valuable skill* ([[brand-narrative]] Act 3). The brief promotes stewardship from subtext to a first-class axis.

## The pairing principle (verbal rule)

Every prestige claim travels with its stewardship counterpart:

| Prestige | Stewardship counterpart |
|---|---|
| Highest entry bar in PolyU | Every capstone serves a real societal issue, with inter-sector partners ([[curriculum]]) |
| Dean-level mentor 1:1 | Mentorship as lineage — what you receive, you owe forward ([[mentorship-and-advising]]) |
| 19 students, 19 degrees | Each pointed at a real-world problem ([[pathways]] C, UNESCO SDGs) |
| ~30 places, global | Selected for judgement and character, not scores alone |

New voice rule for [[voice-and-tone]]: **"Elite about the bar, humble about the person."** Say: judgement, character, stewardship, duty, trust. Avoid: win, dominate, genius, exclusivity-as-benefit; "elite programme" ok, "elite students" never.

## Candidate stewardship lines (for leadership reaction)

1. **"The few, for the many."** ← recommended, campaign-grade
2. "Brilliance is the entry. Character is the degree." (admissions-facing)
3. "Power, kept human." (ties to mission; edgier)
4. "Leaders the future can depend on." (safest)

## Visual encoding

- **Two registers, one identity**: *Ceremonial* (dark ground, declarative voice — admissions, ceremonies, invitations) and *Daylight* (paper ground, first-person voice — mentorship, service, community). Same palette/type/cells; campaigns should show both.
- **Motif library** ("Lego blocks" from the Cell System, see brand-site/themes.html): The Spark & The Horizon (prestige), The Mentor & The Foundation (stewardship), The Ascent & The Cohort (both; Cohort includes "the open seat" ghost cell for recruitment).

## Behavioural proofs

Mostly already exist — promote louder: CUS401 inter-sector capstone, Pathway C SDG curricula, service-learning, CUS201/301 reflections. New suggestions: **The BDSIS Compact** (annual public cohort commitment, signed at orientation, revisited at graduation) and character signals in the selection rubric.

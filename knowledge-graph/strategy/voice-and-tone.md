# Voice and Tone

*Derived by analysing the copy in the brochure and deck — not explicitly defined in any source document. Treat as a proposal to validate with the programme team.*

## Observed voice attributes

1. **Confident, superlative-friendly** — "the most prestigious programme", "most accomplished scholars", "highest entry score requirement". The brand does not hedge its elite claim.
2. **Big-picture and idealistic** — global challenges, future societies, human-centric values; quotes OECD and EUA for authority ([[brand-narrative]]).
3. **Provocative when recruiting attention** — the deck opens with doubt ("I myself also start to doubt the value of a University education") and rhetorical questions ("what can't we learn online?", "so what should we learn now?"). Tension first, resolution after.
4. **Personal and student-centred** — "his/her aspirations", "self-determined", "one-on-one"; student voices speak in first person ([[student-stories]]).
5. **Precise about the mechanics** — credits, codes, percentages are stated exactly; the elite promise is backed by administrable rules ([[curriculum]], [[award-designations]]).

## Tone by context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Taglines/headlines | Aspirational, rhythmic, caps, short ("Breaking Barriers, Empowering Future Leaders.") |
| Why-interdisciplinary storytelling | Urgent, provocative, question-led |
| Programme descriptions | Formal-institutional, complete sentences, British spelling ("programme", "personalised") |
| Student sharing | Warm, first-person, concrete |

## Style conventions observed

- **British English** throughout (programme, personalised, enrol).
- Title-case small-caps headers in collateral ("Programme Aims", "Learning Outcomes").
- Bilingual identity: English-led with Chinese programme name 跨學科組合學士課程 in the logo lockup ([[logo]]); 小红书 channel for Mainland audience ([[digital-channels]]).

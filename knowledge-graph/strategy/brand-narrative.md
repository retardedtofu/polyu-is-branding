# Brand Narrative — the "Why" Story

The colleague-facing deck builds a five-act argument for BDSIS. This narrative arc is brand IP: it should shape recruitment talks, the website "Why" page, and the brand guideline's brand-story section.

## Act 1 — The crisis of confidence in university education (slides 2–6)

- Online courses are everywhere: *"In 2016 we asked 'what can we learn online?' In 2026 we ask 'what can't we learn online?'"*
- Public skepticism about the value of a degree is rising.
- Career aspirations are changing: slashers, one-person start-ups.
- **AI is "levelling up and eating jobs one by one"** — blue collar, white collar, creative media.
- The provocation: *so what should we learn now?*

## Act 2 — Why interdisciplinary, why now (slides 10–17)

- Examples of demanded combinations: Engineering + Business, Hospitality + Psychology, AI + FinTech, Green Finance + Power Engineering, Nursing + MedTech, International Relations + Poverty… (see [[pathways]])
- External authority: OECD (2018) — future-ready students need both broad and specialised knowledge, the capacity to "connect the dots"; EUA (2021) — new discoveries happen at the interface between disciplines.
- **The fundamental driver is technology itself.** Each industrial revolution (steam → electricity → IT/Internet → AI) expanded individual reach and power; increased reach makes other domains relevant → becoming interdisciplinary is the *natural consequence*.
- **"A race between man and machines"** — we created our own competitor and it is moving fast. The answer: teach interdisciplinary knowledge *plus self-learning skills*.

## Act 3 — But knowledge is not enough (slides 18–20)

- A hyper-knowledgeable young CEO still lacks one thing: **trust in their judgement**.
- *"Judgement is the most valuable skill set."* Judgement = wisdom, experience, maturity = a deep, personalised understanding of human behaviour, needs and aspirations, and of how human society forms and works.

## Act 4 — The human foundation at risk (slides 21–23)

- Maslow's hierarchy as a lens on what people live for — and how needs manifest with evolving technology (chatbot companions, addiction).
- Humanity's superpower is **co-operating flexibly in large numbers, enabled by storytelling** (the fundamental pillar of human society).
- *"First we lost our attention to social media. We are now losing storytelling to AI. The foundation of human society is at great risk."*

## Act 5 — The answer: BDSIS (slide 24)

Two complementary dimensions — interdisciplinary capability for a technology-driven world, plus deep human understanding to keep society human-centric. See [[brand-essence]].

## Use in branding

- This arc gives the brand **urgency and moral purpose** — it is not "a flexible degree", it is "educating the leaders who keep society human as AI accelerates".
- The tension *technology ↔ humanity* should be a recurring creative theme; the visual identity already encodes "connection across difference" (see [[graphic-devices]], [[logo]]).

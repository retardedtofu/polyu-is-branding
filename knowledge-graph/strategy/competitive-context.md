# Competitive Context

## Inside PolyU

- PolyU offers numerous undergraduate programmes across **10 faculties/schools** (deck slide 9). BDSIS is positioned **above** them: *"an upgraded version of any PolyU programme"* — not a competitor to individual departments but a premium layer that draws subjects from all of them ([[positioning]]).
- Differentiators vs regular PolyU programmes are tabulated on deck slide 50 (mentors, hall, waived fees, enhanced overseas, switch option) — reproduced in [[key-messages]].
- Internal-politics nuance: the deck reassures colleagues that BDSIS *elevates* their disciplines rather than draining them; departments supply both subjects and [[mentorship-and-advising|mentors]].

## Outside PolyU (implicit)

- The self-determined "strands" structure is **adapted from UCL's pathways** (deck slide 52 notes) — the relevant comparison set is interdisciplinary/liberal-style elite programmes (e.g. UCL Arts & Sciences BASc and HK peers competing for the same top-2%/IB-40+ students).
- Hong Kong context: all universities chase the same small pool of top DSE/IB scorers; BDSIS's weapons are *personalisation depth* (Dean-level mentorship per student) and *PolyU's full subject breadth*.

## Watch-outs for the brand

- "Interdisciplinary" alone is becoming generic in HK higher-ed marketing; the defensible distinctives are the **PAM model**, the **named focus in the award title**, and the **human-centric narrative** ([[brand-narrative]]).
- The elite claim must stay credible: cohort quality stats ([[cohort-2025-26]]) should be refreshed annually in collateral.

Your Mark

Type anything: a name, a student number, a line of poetry. A 128-bit hash turns it into a cell composition on a grid up to 256 × 256, using one lead colour family, a few accent cells, and the quarter, split, circle, and square shapes. Drag the corner of the preview to resize it; the mark keeps its proportions. The same text always produces the same mark. (A business-card version comes later. This is the raw generator.)

How it works: cyrb128 hashes the input to 128 bits, then an sfc32 stream lays out every cell in order (quarter 50%, split 16%, circle 12%, square 10%, empty 12%). The odds of two inputs landing on the same mark are tiny, around 2⁻¹²⁸, and the result is deterministic. The preview is a canvas, so 65,536 cells render instantly; the SVG download shares its gradient definitions to keep files small; PNG output caps at 8192 px.
